Income from the year of death

Income in the final tax year is handled differently depending on whether it was received before or after the date of death.

On this page

  • Which returns you need to file
  • Types of income
  • Get access to the deceased's tax slips

Which returns you need to file

The types of income (lines 10100 to 14600) are the same on the final personal income tax return and the 3 optional returns.

You may choose to report all income in the personal income tax return, but if you choose to file one of the optional returns, you must include all relevant income in that optional return.

Determine if you have eligible income for an optional return.

Income to report in a T3

Depending on the income the person who died received after death, you might be required to file a T3 trust income tax return in addition to the personal income tax return.

Get access to the deceased's tax slips

Most income can be found on the tax slips of the person who died. As the legal or authorised representative, you can gain access to most tax slips through Represent a Client.
