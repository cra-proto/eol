RRIFs

How a registered retirement income fund (RRIF) is treated and reported at the time of the plan holder's (annuitant) death depends on whether there was a rollover or transfer to a survivor or beneficiary.

On this page

  • Report income up to the date of death
  • Remaining value of the RRIF on the date of death and transfers
  • Change a deduction for a decrease in value

Report income up to the date of death

Found on slip
T4RIF - box 16 (issued to the deceased annuitant)
Report on line
11500 of the deceased annuitant's Final return 70(1)

All amounts the annuitant received from the RRIF in the year of death will have to be reported on the annuitant’s Final Return 70(1). A T4RIF slip is issued in the name of the annuitant for the year of death and will also show any other amounts the annuitant received in the year.

If the deceased was 65 or older, or if the deceased was under 65 and received the RRIF payments due to the death of their spouse or common-law partner, see Line 11500 - Other pensions or superannuation.

Remaining value of the RRIF on the date of death and transfers

After the annuitant’s death, the RRIF can be taken over by a spouse or common-law partner, transferred to other qualifying survivors, or left to other beneficiaries or the estate. The reporting requirements depend on how it is transferred.

Determine how the RRIF is being left to beneficiaries or the estate

Who is the RRIF being taken over by or transferred to?

Take over of all or part by a spouse or common-law partner (successor annuitant)

Report annuity payments after the death

Found on slip
T4RIF - box 16 (issued to the spouse or common-law partner)
Report on line
11500 of the spouse or common-law's T1 Income Tax and Benefit return

If the annuitant's spouse or common-law partner is designated as a successor annuitant to all or part of the RRIF in the RRIF contract or the will:

  • the amount of the RRIF that is rolled over to the successor annuitant will not be reported in the Final Return 70(1) of the deceased
  • the spouse or common-law partner will begin receiving amounts paid out from the plan and will report those as part of their own income
Become the successor annuitant if not identified in the RRIF contract

The spouse or common-law partner may become the successor annuitant even if they are not designated as such in the RRIF contract or will if:

  • the deceased's will states that they are entitled to amounts paid under the RRIF, or
  • they are the sole beneficiary of the estate

If either of the criteria above are met, the spouse or common-law partner may write a letter with the legal representative that contains the following:

  • A statement that the spouse or common-law partner and the legal representative jointly elect to treat the amounts paid to the estate as being paid to the spouse or common-law partner
  • A written statement specifying that the spouse or common-law partner elects to become the annuitant of the RRIF
  • The dated signatures of the spouse or common-law partner and the legal representative

Attach a copy of the written election to the T1 Income Tax and Benefit Return of the surviving spouse or common-law partner and provide a copy of the written election to the payer of the annuity.

After this election is made, the spouse or common-law partner will be treated as if they were the beneficiary designated in the RRIF contract.

The spouse or common-law partner as the sole RRIF beneficiary

Eligibility for a full transfer to a spouse or common-law partner

To be eligible to defer tax implications of the RRIF by fully transferring it to the spouse or common-law partner, 2 conditions must be met:

  • The spouse or common-law partner is named as sole beneficiary of the RRIF contract
  • The entire RRIF property is directly transferred, by December 31 of the year following the year of death, to either:
    • the RRSP, PRPP, SPP, or RRIF in which the spouse or common-law partner is the annuitant
    • an issuer to buy an eligible annuity for the spouse or common-law partner

If the full amount is not transferred solely to the spouse or common-law partner, or if the transfer doesn't happen by December 31 of the year following the death, the RRIF will be transferred to the spouse as a qualifying survivor instead.

Transfer is not limited by the usual RRSP deduction limit

The transfer to the spouse or common-law partner's RRSP or RRIF is exempt from their RRSP deduction limit.

When filing their T1 Income Tax and Benefit Return, the spouse or common-law partner should:

  • complete Part C of Schedule 7, RRSP, PRPP, and SPP Unused Contributions, Transfers, and HBP or LLP Activities
  • claim the transfer on line 15 to ensure that the RRSP deduction limit is not reduced by this amount

Report the full transfer

Found on slip
T4RIF - box 16 (issued to the spouse or common-law partner)
Report on line
11500 of the spouse or common-law partner's T1 Income Tax and Benefit return

If the full amount is transferred to the spouse or common-law partner, the RRIF is not reported on the deceased annuitant's Final Return 70(1).

The spouse or common-law partner must report the amount transferred as income on their T1 Income Tax and Benefit return, and may be able to claim a deduction for this amount.

Claim the deduction

The spouse or common-law partner will claim the deduction on the amount transferred on:

  • line 20800 if transferred to their RRSP, PRPP or SPP
  • line 23200 if transferred to their RRIF or used to purchase an annuity

Change in value after the date of death

If there is an increase in the value of the RRIF between the date of death and the transfer, it will be included in box 16. The spouse or common-law partner reports this on line 11500 and claims the deduction on line 20800 or 23200 if applicable.

If there is a decrease in the value of the RRIF between the date of death and the transfer, it will not affect the box 16 amount. Instead, the legal representative may claim a line 23200 deduction on the decrease in value.

One or more qualifying survivors (such as a spouse or grandchild)

Who is considered a qualifying survivor

A deceased annuitant's spouse or common-law partner or financially-dependent children or grandchildren are considered qualifying survivors.

Which children qualify as financially dependent

A child or grandchild of an annuitant, where before the annuitant's death, were living with them and met 1 of the following conditions:

  • net income for the previous year (line 23600 of the T1 Income Tax and Benefit Return) was less than the basic personal amount (line 30000 of the T1 Income Tax and Benefit Return) for that previous year
  • financial dependence is due to a mental or physical infirmity and your net income for the previous year was equal to or less than the basic personal amount, plus the disability amount (line 31600 of the T1 Income Tax and Benefit Return) for that previous year

Transfer is not limited by the usual RRSP deduction limit

Amounts transferred from a deceased annuitant's RRIF to qualifying survivors are not subject to the qualifying survivor's RRSP deduction limits if the transfers:

  • were made to the qualifying survivors' RRSP, PRPP, SPP, RRIF or used to buy them an annuity
  • do not exceed the fair market value of the deceased's RRIF when the annuitant died plus any income earned in the RRIF before December 31 of the year following the death
  • were made during the year or within 60 days of the end of the year that the RRIF was paid out (this is generally only applicable to indirect transfers, such as transfers with form T1090 or annuity purchases after the transfer date)

In addition, if the transfer is made to the qualifying survivor's RRSP or RRIF, it must be:

  • made before December 31 of the year during which the qualifying survivor turns 71

Amounts transferred that meet these criteria are a deductible designated benefit.

If amounts earned in the RRIF after December 31 of the year following the death were included in a transfer, they might not qualify for the deduction (not considered a designated benefit). If this is the case, consult your financial institution about amounts that might not qualify.

When filing their T1 Income Tax and Benefit Return, the qualifying survivor should:

  • complete Part C of Schedule 7, RRSP, PRPP, and SPP Unused Contributions, Transfers, and HBP or LLP Activities
  • claim the qualifying transfer on line 15 to ensure that the RRSP deduction limit is not reduced by this amount

If a transfer does not meet the above conditions, the qualifying survivor might still choose to move the amounts to their registered plans within their deduction limits and claim the usual deduction.

Report the value of the RRIF at the time of death

Found on slip

T4RIF - box 18 (issued to the deceased annuitant)

Report on line

11500 of the deceased annuitant's Final Return 70(1)

At the time of the annuitant’s death, we consider them to have received the fair market value of all property held in their RRIF. This amount will be in box 18 of the T4RIF slip issued to the deceased.

You may reduce the amount of income that is reported on the deceased's Final Return 70(1) if amounts from the RRIF are transferred to the registered plans of qualifying survivors.

How to calculate the reduction to the amount reported in the deceased's return

Reduce the amount from box 18 before reporting it in the Final Return 70(1) by up to:

  • Line 7
  • multiplied by [1 - minus (Line 6 / divided by Line 4)]
  • equals maximum reduction to line 11500 in the deceased's Final Return 70(1)

Where:

Lines to calculate reduction in deceased's Final Return 70(1)
Calculation line Definition
Line 4
  • The fair market value of the RRIF on the later of the following two dates:
    • December 31 of the year after the year the annuitant died
    • the end of the day the last time a designated benefit was paid out of the RRIF
  • plus The total of all amounts paid or transferred out of the RRIF after the annuitant died
  • equals Line 4
Line 6
  • Line 4
  • minus Box 18 of the T4RIF slip issued to the deceased annuitant
  • equals Line 6 (if Line 6 would be negative, make it 0)
Line 7
  • Amount designated as a designated benefit on each Form T1090 filed for the RRIF
  • plus The part of the amounts shown in box 36 of all T4RIF slips and box 13 of all T5 slips issued in the name of the estate that the qualifying survivors are entitled to receive from the estate
  • plus Amounts shown in box 16 and 22 of all T4RIF slips and box 13 of all T5 slips issued to qualifying survivors
  • plus The part of the amount shown in box 36 of all T4RIF slips that were issued to the qualifying survivors that does not have to be included in income (contact the deceased annuitant’s RRIF issuer to determine these amounts)
  • plus The part of the amount shown in box 18 of the T4RIF slip that was issued to the deceased annuitant for the year of death and that the qualifying survivors are entitled to receive
  • equals Line 7

Refer to Chart 2 in the PDF version of RC4178 Death of an RRIF Annuitant or a PRPP member for more details.

Report the amount transferred to a qualifying survivor

Found on slip

T4RIF - box 22 (issued to the qualifying survivor)

or

T4RIF - box 16 (instead of box 22 if the qualifying survivor is the spouse or common-law partner)

Report on line

11500 of the qualifying survivor's T1 Income Tax and Benefit return

or

13000 of the qualifying survivor's T1 Income Tax and Benefit return

The qualifying survivor must report the amount transferred in full on:

  • line 11500 if the funds were received due to the annuitant's death, or if the qualifying survivor was 65 or older when the transfer was received
  • line 13000 if the funds were received other than due to the death, or if the qualifying survivor was 64 or younger when the transfer was received

Claim deductions for transfers to qualifying survivors

The qualifying survivor will claim the deduction on the designated benefit (generally, the same as the amount transferred) on:

  • line 20800 if transferred to their RRSP, PRPP or SPP
  • line 23200 if transferred to their RRIF or used to purchase an annuity

Attach the receipt for the transfer of funds issued by the bank or other issuer to the return. This is to ensure the transfer meets the requirements for the deduction.

Change in value after the date of death

If there is an increase in the value of the RRIF between the date of death and the transfer, the increase will be included in box 22 or box 16. The qualifying survivor reports this on line 13000 and claims the deduction on line 20800 or line 23200 if applicable.

If there is a decrease in the value of the RRIF between the date of death and the transfer, it will not be included in the box 22 or box 16 amount. Instead, the legal representative may claim a line 23200 deduction on the decrease in value.

If the RRIF is not transferred until after December 31 of the year following the death

Income of the RRIF after December 31 of the year following the death is reported differently for different banks and financial institutions. Ask the financial institution managing the RRIF if it is a depositary, trusteed or insured RRIF, and refer to the third column in Chart 1 of the RC4178 Death of a RRIF Annuitant or a PRPP member about how to report it.

Amounts earned in a depositary or trusteed RRIF after December 31 of the year following the death do not qualify to be part of the line 20800 or line 23200 deductions.

A child or grandchild's RDSP

Which individuals are eligible

An individual is eligible for the rollover to an RDSP if they are:

  • a child or grandchild of a deceased annuitant
  • the beneficiary under the RDSP into which the eligible proceeds will be paid
  • financially dependent on the deceased for support, at the time of the deceased's death, because of an impairment in physical or mental function
Which children qualify as financially dependent

A child or grandchild of an annuitant, where before the annuitant's death, were living with them and met 1 of the following conditions:

  • net income for the previous year (line 23600 of the T1 Income Tax and Benefit Return) was less than the basic personal amount (line 30000 of the T1 Income Tax and Benefit Return) for that previous year
  • financial dependence is due to a mental or physical infirmity and your net income for the previous year was equal to or less than the basic personal amount, plus the disability amount (line 31600 of the T1 Income Tax and Benefit Return) for that previous year

Transfer the RRIF amount to the RDSP

The payment into the RDSP must meet all of the following conditions:

  • The payment is made to an RDSP under which the eligible individual is the beneficiary
  • The payment does not cause the RDSP to exceed the $200,000 lifetime limit of the RDSP and is made before the RDSP holder turns 59
  • If the eligible individual no longer qualifies for the disability tax credit, the payment is made before the end of the fifth taxation year throughout which the beneficiary is ineligible for the disability tax credit

You will not have to fill out a Schedule 7, RRSP, PRPP, and SPP Unused Contributions, Transfers, and HBP or LLP Activities.

Report amounts in the deceased's Final Return 70(1)

Was the eligible individual designated as the beneficiary in the RRIF contract or will?

Yes - beneficiary designated in the RRIF contract or will

Amounts rolled over to an eligible individual's RDSP are not reported on the deceased annuitant's Final Return 70(1).

No - beneficiary designated by a joint election with form T1090

If the beneficiary was not designated in the RRIF contract or the will, you might need to report the amount from box 22 of the T4RIF that was transferred to the RDSP on line 13000 of the deceased's Final Return 70(1).

In this case, a line 23200 deduction may be claimed for the amount rolled over on the deceased's Final Return 70(1).

Report the amount rolled over

Found on slip
T4RIF - box 22 (issued to the eligible individual)
Report on line
13000 of the eligible individual's T1 Income Tax and Benefit return

The eligible individual must report the amount rolled over in full on line 13000 of their T1 Income Tax and Benefit return.

A completed RC4625, Rollover to a Registered Disability Savings Plan (RDSP) Under Paragraph 60(m) must be attached to both the deceased annuitant's and the eligible qualifying survivor's T1 Income Tax and Benefit Returns.

Claim the deduction

Claim a deduction on line 23200 equal to the amount rolled over to the RDSP.

You must attach to the T1 Income Tax and Benefit Return the receipt from the bank or other issuer for the transfer indicating the amount rolled over.

Ineligible for Canada Disability Savings Grants

Rolled over amounts will not be eligible for any Canada Disability Savings Grants.

Other beneficiaries or the estate

Report the value of the RRIF at the time of death

Found on slip
T4RIF - box 18 (issued to the deceased annuitant)
Report on line
13000 of the deceased annuitant's Final Return 70(1)

At the time of the annuitant’s death, we consider them to have received the fair market value of all property held in their RRIF. This amount will be in box 18 of the T4RIF slip issued to the deceased annuitant.

This amount and all other amounts the deceased annuitant received from the RRIF in the year of death have to be reported on line 13000 of the annuitant’s Final Return 70(1).

Payments to beneficiaries of the estate

As the amount is reported as part of the deceased's Final Return 70(1), there is no requirement for beneficiaries of amounts paid from the deceased's RRIF to report the amount received in their own tax returns.

If there is no beneficiary, the RRIF property is generally paid out to the deceased annuitant's estate.

Claim a deduction for amounts transferred to a qualifying survivor

If a beneficiary of the estate who receives an amount from the RRIF reported in the Final Return 70(1) is a qualifying survivor, you may want to consider designating the amount of the RRIF transferred from the estate to the qualifying survivor as a designated benefit:

  1. The annuitant's legal representative and the qualifying survivor jointly file Form T1090 - Designated Benefit or Joint Designation on the Death of a PRPP Member
  2. Line 13000 of the deceased's Final Return 70(1) should be reduced by the amount designated, if the transfer meets the requirements to be considered deductible
  3. The amount transferred to the qualifying survivor is then deducted from income in the qualifying survivor's return, if the transfer meets the requirements to be considered deductible

Increase in value after the date of death

Found on slip
T4RIF - box 22 (issued to a beneficiary or the estate)
Report on line

13000 of the beneficiary's T1 Income Tax and Benefit Return

or

11 of the estate's T3 Trust Income Tax and Information return

If there is an increase in the value of the RRIF after the date of death, a T4RIF slip will be issued to the beneficiary or the estate who are considered to receive the income and should report it on their return.

Decrease in value after the date of death

If there is a decrease in the value of the RRIF between the date of death and distribution to beneficiaries, the legal representative may claim a line 23200 deduction on the decrease in value.

Claim a deduction for a decrease in value

Sometimes, the fair market value of the property of an RRIF decreases between the date of death and the date of final distribution to the beneficiary or the estate.

  • If the total of all distributions from the RRIF is less than the fair market value of the property that was included in the deceased annuitant's income for the year of death, the deceased's legal representative can request that the difference be deducted on the deceased's Final Return 70(1) through a reassessment
  • Form RC249 - Post-Death Decline in the Value of a RRIF, an Unmatured RRSP and Post-Death Increase or Decline in the Value of a PRPP can be validated by the RRIF issuer and will allow the executor to claim a deduction on line 23200
  • Generally, for the deduction to be allowed, the final distribution must occur by the end of the year that follows the year of death
