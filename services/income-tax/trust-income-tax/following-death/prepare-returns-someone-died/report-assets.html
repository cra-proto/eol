Get access to the deceased's tax slips

Most income can be found on the tax slips of the person who died. As the legal or authorised representative, you can get access to most tax slips through Represent a Client.

Determine if earned before or after the date of death

  • Before date of death: Option 1

    Any income received received by someone who died between January 1 and the date of death must be reported in the final personal income tax return 70(1).

    If the income was earned between January 1 and the date of death but only paid after the date of death, it must be reported on either the final personal income tax return 70(1) or an optional return for rights or things 70(2).

  • After date of death: Option 2

    If the estate received income after the date of death such as interest that accumulates after the death, it generally must be reported on a T3 Trust Income and Information Return.

Types of income on the final personal and optional returns

The income lines are the same on the final personal income tax return as well as the optional returns. However, the T3 Trust Income and Information Return has different line items for similar income types.

If you choose not to file the optional returns, simply include all income in the personal return 70(1) and, if required, a T3 trust return for income after death. If you would like to file the optional returns, refer to how to report income on the optional returns.

How to report income on the optional returns

The optional returns use the same T1 return form as the final personal return, and have the same income line numbers as the final personal return. Learn how to file the optional returns.

The difference is that you can only report income from specific sources and time periods in the optional returns. They are as follows:

Return for rights or things 70(2)

You can only report income that was received after death that, had the person not died, would have been included in their usual personal income tax return.

Types of eligible income
  • salary, commissions, and vacation pay owed before the death and paid after death
    • owed to the deceased by the employer on the date of death for a pay period that ended before the date of death
  • retroactive salary adjustments owed and paid after death
  • Old Age Security (OAS), Canada Pension Plan/Quebec Pension Plan (CPP/QPP) paid after the date of death for the month of death
  • CPP and Employment Insurance (EI) arrears
  • accounts receivable, supplies, and inventory
    • accounts receivable, supplies on hand, and inventory are rights or things if the deceased's business used the cash method. The inventory of an artist who has elected to value their inventory at nil is also a rights or things.
  • uncashed matured bond coupons
  • bond interest earned but not received before death
  • dividends declared before the date of death, but not received
  • crops, livestock
    • this includes harvested farm crops and livestock that is not part of the basic herd. For more information, see Archived Interpretation Bulletins IT234, Income of Deceased Persons - Farm Crops, and IT427R,  Livestock of Farmers.
  • work in progress
    • work in progress is a right or thing if the deceased was a sole proprietor and a professional (accountant, dentist, lawyer [in Quebec an advocate or notary], medical doctor, veterinarian, or chiropractor) who had elected to exclude work in progress when calculating their total income. For more information about rights or things, see Archived Interpretation Bulletin IT212R3, Income of Deceased Persons - Rights or things, and its Special Release.
Return for a partner or proprietor 150(4)

If the person who died was a sole proprietor or partner with a fiscal year end that is not December 31, you may report in the optional return any income received between the fiscal year end and the date of death.

Example of eligible income for a proprietor

A person who had a business died on May 28, 2021. The business has a March 31 fiscal year-end.

You have two choices when you report the person's 2021 income:

  • include the business income from April 1, 2020, to May 28, 2021 on the final return.
  • file a return for a partner or proprietor in addition to the final return.
    • On the final return, include business income from April 1, 2020, to March 31, 2021.
    • On the return for a partner or proprietor, report the business income from April 1, 2021, to May 28, 2021.
Return for a Graduated Rate Estate (GRE) 104(23)(d)

If income was received from a Graduated Rate Estate of another person who died (generally found on a T3 slip) between the fiscal year end of the GRE and the date of death.

Example of income from a GRE

A husband gets income from a testamentary trust with a fiscal year from April 1 to March 31. The trust was formed as a result of his wife's death on March 31, 2020 and designates itself as a GRE of the wife in its return of income for March 31, 2021. The husband died on June 11, 2021.

You have two choices when you report the husband's income from the trust:

  • Include the husband's income from the GRE from April 1, 2020, to June 11, 2021, on his final return.
  • File a return for income received from the GRE in addition to the final return.
    • On the husband's final return, include the income from the GRE from April 1, 2020, to March 31, 2021.
    • On the optional return for income from the GRE, report the income from April 1, 2021, to June 11, 2021.

Income on a T3 return

Depending on the income the person who died received after death, you might be required to file a T3 trust income tax return in addition to the personal income tax return.

You might need to file T3 returns for more than one year, if the estate continues to receive income in tax years after the year of death.
