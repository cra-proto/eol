Represent someone who died

When someone dies, the responsibility of filing a final return and ensuring debts are resolved falls on their legal representative.

As the legal representative, you are authorised to access the tax records of the deceased and file returns on their behalf.

The legal representative can also authorize a representative, such as an accountant, to help them prepare tax returns.

Find out if you're the legal representative

Person who died is a resident of Quebec

If the deceased was a resident of the province of Quebec, find out if you’re the legal representative with Revenu Québec.

Person dies with a will

If an executor is named in the will, the executor of the will is the legal representative for the person who died.

Person dies without a will

If a person dies without a will (intestate), or there is no executor named in the will, a spouse, common-law partner or next of kin will usually be appointed by the court to handle the deceased’s estate.

If you are in this situation, you may request to be the deceased person's legal representative by completing an Affidavit form for intestate situations.

Responsibilities of the legal representative

As the legal representative you should:

  1. Contact the CRA about the death
  2. Ensure you keep records for the person who died:
    • a copy of the death certificate
    • a complete copy of the will or other legal document such as a grant of probate or letters of administration showing that you are the legal representative
    • the mailing address of the estate
    • if not already sent, a completed Request for the Canada Revenue Agency to Update Records form the deceased individual’s social insurance number (SIN), which should be sent with any request or documents to the CRA
  3. File any tax returns for the person who died
  4. Ensure debts of the estate are resolved

As the legal representative, you can register to represent a client or authorize another representative to do so.

Authorize an accountant or other representative

An authorized representative is someone who is able to access the tax records of the person who died.

As the legal representative, you may wish to appoint an authorized representative to deal with the CRA for tax matters on your behalf.

For more information go to Representative authorization.

  • Authorized representative income

    Unless included in your business income, trustee, executor, or liquidator fees paid to you for acting as an executor is income from an office or employment. As the executor, you must report these fees on a T4 slip.

    For more information, see Employment by a trustee in Chapter 1 of the T4001, Employers' Guide - Payroll Deductions and Remittances.

    • Register as a representative and get a representative ID

    Register for Represent a Client or contact the CRA to get access to get a representative identifier (RepID) and get access to the tax records of the person who died

    Make sure to provide your RepID, in addition to the deceased’s SIN, when you are submitting tax returns.

    Both legal representatives and other authorized representatives can sign up for access through Represent a Client.
