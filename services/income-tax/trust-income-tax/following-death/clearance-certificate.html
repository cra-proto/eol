Why you need a clearance certificate

Having a clearance certificate confirms that an estate of a person who died or a corporation has paid all amounts of tax, interest and penalties it owed at the time the certificate was issued.

The legal representative may apply for a clearance certificate when they need to distribute assets without the risk of being personally responsible for amounts the person who died, estate, trust or corporation might owe the CRA. Your financial institution or lawyers may also ask you for a clearance certificate.

Depending on your situation, determine if you should apply for a clearance certificate:

For an estate of someone who died

You will need a clearance certificate if:

  • you need to distribute assets of a deceased individual
  • the person had a GST/HST number

You will not be able to get a clearance certificate as long as the estate remains in debt to the CRA. Learn about how to manage an estate that cannot pay its debts.

For corporations

You will need clearance certificate if:

  • the corporation is winding up or for any other type of corporate dissolution and you are planning to distribute corporate property

If the corporation will continue operations, instead of a clearance certificate you may only require a comfort letter.

For a trust

You will need a clearance certificate if:

  • the trust is winding up or for any other type of corporate dissolution and plan to distribute corporate assets
  • the estate of someone who died is acting as a trust and has assets to distribute
For GST/HST

You will need a clearance certificate if there is a business with a GST number

Distributing assets without a clearance certificate

If the legal representative does not get a clearance certificate before they distribute assets and there are any tax amounts owing to the CRA, they are personally liable for unpaid amounts, up the value of the amount of assets distributed.

When to apply for a clearance certificate

Do not submit your clearance certificate request at the same time as you file your returns. This will result in a delay assessing your returns.

A clearance certificate should be requested after:

  • all of the returns have been filed
  • you have received the notices of assessment from the returns
  • any outstanding balances owing to the CRA have been paid and there are no other outstanding adjustment requests

Prepare your request

If you have not previously sent us the supporting documents, submit them with your completed form.

Forms and supporting documents you need

For an estate of someone who has died

You will need to fill in a TX19 Asking for a Clearance Certificate form.

If the person who died also had a GST/HST number, you will also need to fill out the form GST352 Application for Clearance Certificate.

Supporting documents:

  • A complete and signed copy of the taxpayer's will, including any codicils, renunciations, disclaimers, and all probate documents if applicable. If the taxpayer died intestate (without a will), attach a copy of the document appointing an administrator (for example, the Letters of Administration or Letters of Verification issued by a provincial court)
  • Any other documents that are necessary to prove that you are the legal representative
  • A detailed list of the assets that were owned by the person who died at the date of death
  • A statement of proposed distribution of any holdback or residual amount or property
  • The names, addresses, and social insurance numbers of any beneficiaries of property other than cash
  • A completed Form T1013, Authorizing or Cancelling a Representative, signed by all legal representatives, authorizing a representative such as an accountant, notary, or lawyer if you want us to communicate with any other person or firm, or if you want the clearance certificate sent to any address other than your own
For corporations

You will need to fill in a TX19 Asking for a Clearance Certificate form.

If the business also has a GST/HST account, you will also need to fill out the form GST352 Application for Clearance Certificate.

Supporting documents:

  • A copy of the director’s or shareholder’s resolution confirming the intention to dissolve the corporation and the date of dissolution
  • A copy of the notice of assessment for the final T2 filed
  • any other documents that are necessary to prove that you are the legal representative
  • A completed Form RC59, Business Consent, signed by all legal representatives, authorizing a representative such as an accountant or lawyer if you want us to communicate with any other person or firm, or if you want the clearance certificate sent to any address other than your own
  • A statement of distribution of the company assets to date as well as the scheme of the distribution of company assets at the date of wind-up
For trusts

You will need to fill in a TX19 Asking for a Clearance Certificate form.

If the person who died also had a GST/HST number, you will also need to fill out the form GST352 Application for Clearance Certificate.

Supporting documents:

  • A copy of the trust agreement or documents for inter vivos trusts
  • Any other documents that are necessary to prove that you are the legal representative
  • A statement of distribution of the trust assets to date as well as the scheme of the distribution of trust assets at the date of wind-up
  • The names, addresses, and social insurance numbers of any beneficiaries of property other than cash
For GST/HST

You will need to complete Form GST352, Application for Clearance Certificate

Send in your request

Once you have prepared your request, submit it to us by one of the following methods:

Online:
Represent A Client

Submit the completed form Asking for a Clearance Certificate (TX19, GST352) and any supporting information through Represent a Client.

Representatives do not need to have electronic authorization access to the specific clients account to submit clearance certificate requests via Represent a Client.

  1. Log into Represent a Client.
  2. Select the “Submit Documents” from the options on the left hand side of the screen.
  3. Select Client Type:
    • Individual Client (T1)
    • Business Client (T2) (GST/HST)
    • Trust Account (T3)
    • Representative related: Documents on behalf of yourself as a representative
  4. Select “No” to the question “Do you have a case or reference number?”.
  5. Click on the “Next” button.
  6. Select “Send request for Clearance Certificate (TX19, GST352), and supporting documents”.
  7. Select “Next”.
  8. Select “Browse” and navigate to the required file(s).
    • Multiple files totalling up to 500MB can be attached by pressing and holding the CTRL key while selecting your files. A 10 file limit per session also applies.
  9. Enter a description of the file submission. This is a mandatory step.
  10. Select “Attach file”.
  11. When all files have been attached, click “Submit file(s)”.
  12. Record the Confirmation number and Case/reference number.

For help with income tax clearance certificates, call 1-800-959-8281.

For help with goods and services tax/harmonized sales tax (GST/HST) or T2 clearance certificates, call 1-800-959-5525.

Processing times

We will send the representative of the person who died an acknowledgement letter within 45 days of receiving the request for a clearance certificate.

Our assessment can take up to 120 days, assuming all of the necessary documents were provided. However, in certain situations, the CRA may need to do an audit before it issues the clearance certificate.

If your request is denied, you will be advised by what information is missing and what still needs to be done. You will be able to reapply for clearance certificate once you have addressed these issues.

If you have not received your certificate, have been denied a certificate or a clearance certificate officer has not contacted you within 120 days, contact us

Contact us at the number for the type of clearance certificate you are applying for:

  • For individual (T1), or trust (T3) accounts call 1-800-959-8281
  • For corporations (T2) or GST/HST accounts call 1-800-959-5525

After you receive a clearance certificate

When issuing a clearance certificate the CRA reviews all known information available to verify all balances owing have been paid.

Once the legal representative has received a clearance certificate, they are then able to distribute assets without the risk of being personally liable for amounts the person who died, estate, trust or corporation might owe the CRA.

If new debts are discovered after a clearance certificate is issued

A clearance certificate separates the personal liability of the legal representative from any newly discovered debts after the certificate has been issued. Once the legal representative has a clearance certificate, liability then rests on the estate, trust, corporation, beneficiaries, or other people who have received funds from the estate, trust, corporation, or GST/HST account.
