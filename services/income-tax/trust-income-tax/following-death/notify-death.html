Call or mail the date of death as soon as possible

Contact us as soon as possible to avoid any repayment situations for benefits. Even if the deceased was not receiving benefit payments, you should report the date of death as the legal representative.

By phone:

Before you call

To verify your identity, you'll need your:

  • full name
  • phone number
  • relation to the person who died

In addition to your information, you will need the following information about the person who died:

  • date of death
  • social insurance number (SIN)
  • full name and date of birth
  • complete address
  • assessed tax return, notice of assessment or reassessment, other tax document

By mail:
Complete a form to notify us by mail

Complete the applicable information in the form on the back page of the PDF version of Information Sheet RC4111, What to do following a death and send it to your tax services office or tax centre.

You may need to send in other completed forms to confirm you are the legal representative and get access to tax records or authorize another representative.

Additional help to cancel or transfer benefits

Generally, by reporting the date of death to the CRA, benefits payments will be appropriately managed, however, in some cases, you might need to take further steps to ensure they are cancelled or transferred:

GST/HST credit

If the person who died was receiving GST/HST credit payments, the CRA may still send out a payment after the date of death because they are not aware of the death. If this happens, you should return the payment to the tax centre that serves your area.

Deceased was single, separated, divorced, or widowed

If the recipient died before the scheduled month in which the CRA issues the GST/HST credit, payments will no longer be issued in that person’s name or to that person’s estate.

If the recipient died during or after the scheduled month in which the CRA issues the credit and the payment has not been cashed, return it to the CRA so that they can send the payment to the person's estate.

If the person who died was getting a credit for a child, the child's new caregiver should contact the CRA at 1-800-387-1193, as they may qualify to receive GST/HST credit payments for that child.

Deceased received the credit for both themselves and their spouse or common-law partner

The deceased's spouse or common-law partner may now be eligible to receive the GST/HST credit if they filed an income tax and benefit return. The GST/HST credit payments will be based on their net income alone.

Deceased's spouse or common-law partner received the credit for both themselves and the deceased

The payments will be recalculated based on the surviving spouse's or common-law partner's net income and will only include a claim for them and any eligible children, if applicable.

Deceased is an eligible child for whom the credit was paid

Entitlement to GST/HST credit payments for a child who died stops the quarter after the child's date of death. You should notify the CRA of the date of death so that the CRA can update their records.

Canada Workers Benefit (CWB) advance payments

A deceased person with a date of death between January 1 and June 30 is not eligible for the Canada Workers Benefit (CWB) in the year of death.

If the deceased received advance payments, a repayment might need to be made.

The benefit for the final year up to the death must be claimed on line 45300 in the T1 final return for the deceased.

Canada Child Benefit (CCB) payments for a child

If the person who died was receiving CCB payments, including payments from related provincial or territorial child benefit and credit programs, and the surviving spouse or common-law partner is the child’s parent, the CRA will usually transfer the CCB payments to that person.

Apply as a new recipient of the benefit who is not the parent

If anyone other than the parent, is now primarily responsible for the care and upbringing of the child, that person will have to apply for benefit payments for the child through one of the following options:

  • By using the "Apply for child benefits" online service on My Account
  • By completing and sending to the CRA Form RC66, Canada Child Benefits Application

If the person who died was receiving payments under provincial or territorial child benefit and credit programs administered by the CRA, there is no need to apply separately to qualify. The CRA will use the information from the application to determine the new caregiver's eligibility for these programs.

Deceased's spouse or common-law partner received the benefit for both themselves and the deceased

If the surviving spouse or common-law partner receives CCB payments, the CRA will automatically recalculate the payments excluding the net income of the person who died when the CRA is notified of the death.

Deceased is an eligible child for whom the benefit was paid

Eligibility for CCB payments stops after the child's date of death.
